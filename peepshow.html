PEEPSHOW FACIAL RECOGNITION ILLUSTRATION

Peepshow is an interactive Illustration I made as a personal project. I wanted to think of a way I could make my illustrations more engaging by adding an element of interactivity — the clothes only come off when there's no one looking.

Try it out for yourself.

SKETCHING

I started by practicing my sketching skills by spending almost a full week sketching nudes — I'd never sketched human-form before, so it took many attempts and a lot of critique from my mentors Prakash Chhetri and Rowan Newton until I got something I was happy with.

Early sketches of a dubious quality

DIGITAL ILLUSTRATION

My final sketches were then vectorised in Illustrator and textures and shading were added in Photoshop. I tried to develop my own visual style, based on previous illustrations of mine, visual references, and input from my mentors.

TECHNICAL SOLUTION

I then moved on to the task of making the illustrations interactive using facial recognition. I first made a prototype in MaxMSP, and then finally in JavaScript, where I modified existing code and made use of a facial recognition library.
